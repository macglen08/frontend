Balashaheb Thackeray

1926-2012

An Politician

Bal Keshav Thackeray, lovingly known addressed as 'Balasaheb', was a cartoonist, Indian journalist and politician, founder of a political party named as "Shivsena", a right-wing pro-Marathi and Hindu nationalist party mainly active in the state of Maharashtra.



Career

Thackeray started his career as a cartoonist in Mumbai in the 1950s. He worked for the Free Press Journal and his cartoons were published in the Times of India's Sunday edition.
Thackeray launched his cartoon weekly "Marmik" along with his brother Shrikant in 1960. His cartoons also appeared in the Sunday edition of New York Times and Japanese daily newspaper.
He was encouraged to lay a foundation of the political party "Shiv Sena". The meaning of Shiv Sena is the 'Army of Shivaji', a great Maratha Warrior and this party works for Marathi Manus(Marathi People).
This party emerged as one of the prominent parties of the state and gave three Chief Ministers to the state.
In 1989, Thackeray launched party's newspaper,"Saamna".

Controversies

  • For the first time in 1966, he dragged the attention of media by campaigning against South Indians who were snatching jobs in Mumbai from Marathi people.
  • During the Maharashtra-Karnataka border dispute in 1969, he was arrested and sent to Yerwada Central jail in Pune, Maharashtra.
  • He was a strong critic of Pakistan in 1998, during the concert of famous Pakistani singer Ghulam Ali in Mumbai.
  • During the Babri Mosque demolition in December 1992, he along with other hardliners was accused of the mishappening.
  • In 1995, he objected to the release of the Bollywood film "Bombay" which was based on Bombay Bomb-blast of 1993.
  • He once hit out at the then President of India, APJ Abdul Kalam, for not pronouncing the death sentence to Afzal Guru.
